How the QARP framework works

Every stock clears two gates — first a Shariah screen (pass/fail), then a quality-and-value score. Here's what the numbers mean.

QARP Quality At a Reasonable Price

A single 0–100 score that blends how good a business is with how reasonably it's priced — 60% quality, 40% value. Higher is better.

QARP = 60% × Quality + 40% × DCF value

DCF score 1 – 5

How cheap a stock looks versus an estimate of its fair value (triangulated from several models). 5 = deep bargain, 1 = expensive.

4Undervalued+15% to +30%
3Fairly priced−10% to +15%

The Shariah screen the first gate

Every name must pass an AAOIFI-standard screen before it's ever scored — verified primarily via Musaffa and confirmed by Muslim Xchange. If they disagree, the stricter verdict wins. A non-compliant company gets no QARP score and is excluded.

  • Permissible core business (no interest/lending, alcohol, tobacco, pork, gambling, adult content, weapons)
  • Impermissible income < 5% of revenue
  • Interest-bearing debt < 30% of market cap
  • Interest-bearing cash & securities < 30% of market cap

Informational analysis, not investment advice. Scores are a snapshot at the stated date and price — a name can be a buy when cheap and an avoid after it runs up.
