The goal

To navigate the US stock market with knowledge instead of guesswork — staying deeply informed, sidestepping overpriced names, and putting capital preservation first. Every stock is screened for Shariah compliance, then scored on its quality and its price. Below is the framework that turns that discipline into a single number.

QARP Quality At a Reasonable Price

One 0–100 score from two inputs: Quality — how strong the business is, out of 105 — counts 60%; DCF value — how cheap it is vs. fair value, 1 to 5 — counts 40%. Higher is better. Drag the sliders to see how they combine.

The Shariah screen the first gate · pass / fail

Every name must pass an AAOIFI-standard screen before it's ever scored — verified via Musaffa and confirmed by Muslim Xchange (the stricter view wins). A non-compliant company gets no QARP score and is excluded.

  • Permissible core business (no interest/lending, alcohol, tobacco, pork, gambling, adult content, weapons)
  • Impermissible income < 5% of revenue
  • Interest-bearing debt < 30% of market cap
  • Interest-bearing cash & securities < 30% of market cap
Verdict bands STRONGEST ≥85 STRONG BUY ≥72 BUY ≥66 HOLD-QUAL ≥60 AVOID 35–59 STRONG AVOID <35

Informational analysis, not investment advice. Scores are a snapshot at the stated date and price — a name can be a buy when cheap and an avoid after it runs up.
