The goal

To navigate the US stock market with knowledge instead of guesswork — staying deeply informed, sidestepping overpriced names, and putting capital preservation first. Every stock is screened for Shariah compliance, then scored on its quality and its price. Below is the framework that turns that discipline into a single number.

QARP Quality At a Reasonable Price

One 0–100 score from two inputs — Quality (how strong the business is, out of 105) and DCF value (how cheap it is vs. fair value, 1–5). Higher is better. The blend isn’t fixed — it depends on company size: big companies split 60 / 40, and the smaller a company gets, the more weight shifts onto Quality, up to 75 / 25 (the next card explains why). Pick a size and drag the sliders — the same numbers score differently.

How the blend changes by company size why a small-cap is judged differently

Big companies are heavily analyzed, so their DCF value read is reliable and earns the full 40%. Smaller companies are systematically lower-quality and their cash-flow forecasts are far noisier — so the weight slides toward business quality & balance-sheet strength and away from the standalone value estimate. It’s grounded in the research on size, profitability, and DCF reliability: down the size scale, surviving and compounding beats chasing a cheap-looking price.

Same business, different score. A name with Quality 78 and Value 3 scores ~69 (BUY) as a large-cap but ~70 as a small-cap — the small-cap is rewarded more for its quality. Flip it: a cheap-but-fragile name (low quality, high value score) loses ground as it shrinks, because a low price no longer rescues a weak balance sheet. The weight slides continuously with size; the bands here are illustrative, and large caps are unchanged from the original 60 / 40.

The Shariah screen the first gate · pass / fail

Every name must pass an AAOIFI-standard screen before it's ever scored — verified via Musaffa and confirmed by Muslim Xchange (the stricter view wins). A non-compliant company gets no QARP score and is excluded.

  • Permissible core business (no interest/lending, alcohol, tobacco, pork, gambling, adult content, weapons)
  • Impermissible income < 5% of revenue
  • Interest-bearing debt < 30% of market cap
  • Interest-bearing cash & securities < 30% of market cap
